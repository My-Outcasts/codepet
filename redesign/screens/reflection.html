YOU ASKED
Why does my streak reset at midnight even when I coded earlier that day?
BYTE · NARRATOR

Ah, a sneaky one. Your streak wasn't lying to you — it was just reading the wrong clock. Every night at midnight the kingdom checked whether you'd visited "today," but it measured the day in UTC while you live and code in your own timezone.

So for anyone west of London, a late-afternoon session looked like it belonged to tomorrow that hadn't started yet, and the streak quietly slipped back to zero.

Technical details: the comparison used Calendar(identifier: .gregorian) with the default UTC timezone instead of Calendar.current.
YOU ASKED
How do I make the day boundary follow the user's local time?
BYTE · NARRATOR

We handed the calendar a compass. By anchoring it to the device's own timezone, "today" finally meant your today — sunrise to sunrise where you actually sit.

Then we compared the stored last-active date against that local day. One quiet line of code, and the midnight gremlin lost its power for good.

Technical details: switched to calendar.isDateInYesterday(lastActive) and set calendar.timeZone = .current in StreakManager.swift.
